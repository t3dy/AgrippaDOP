Book III — Ceremonial Magic — Chapter 35

Of the Purification of the Soul for Prophetic Work

New in 1533

Perrone Compagni — Table of Comparison

1533 edition (3:35): new (title not recorded in the Tables of Contents at­ tached to A; henceforth numbering of following chapters in A differs from K by two)

This chapter, 3:35 (“Of the Purification of the Soul for Prophetic Work”), occupies a significant place in Agrippa's systematic exposition of occult philosophy. In the 1533 Cologne printed edition (De occulta philosophia libri tres, sigla K in Perrone Compagni's critical apparatus), the text represents Agrippa's mature formulation of the ideas it addresses, the product of over two decades of reading, travel, and revision since the composition of the Würzburg draft.

According to Perrone Compagni's table of comparison, this chapter is new in the 1533 edition and has no direct counterpart in the Würzburg manuscript (W = MS M.ch.q.50). The description notes: \u201c(title not recorded in the Tables of Contents at­ tached to A; henceforth numbering of following chapters in A differs from K by two).\u201d Its addition reflects the conceptual enrichment Agrippa undertook between approximately 1510 and the early 1530s, incorporating sources and arguments unavailable or unexplored in the juvenile draft.

The absence of this chapter from the Würzburg draft invites reflection on what prompted its insertion in the mature edition. Agrippa's intellectual biography between 1510 and 1530 — including his engagement with Neoplatonism, Kabbalah, Hermetism, and scholastic natural philosophy — provides the context for understanding such additions. The partial edition A (sigla A in Perrone Compagni) may offer clues about when the material was composed.
