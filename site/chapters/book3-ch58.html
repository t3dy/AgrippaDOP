Book III — Ceremonial Magic — Chapter 58

Concerning the Ablutions and Purifications of the Magician

De adorationibus et votis
Heavily Expanded

Perrone Compagni — Table of Comparison

1533 edition (3:58): W, 3: 45 + many additions

This chapter, De adorationibus et votis (“Concerning the Ablutions and Purifications of the Magician”), occupies a significant place in Agrippa's systematic exposition of occult philosophy. In the 1533 Cologne printed edition (De occulta philosophia libri tres, sigla K in Perrone Compagni's critical apparatus), the text represents Agrippa's mature formulation of the ideas it addresses, the product of over two decades of reading, travel, and revision since the composition of the Würzburg draft.

This chapter derives from W 3:45 of the Würzburg draft, but has been substantially expanded — Perrone Compagni notes many additions. The scale of interpolation suggests Agrippa rethought and broadened the original argument considerably; the 1533 text is in essence a new development of the earlier material rather than a simple revision.

The extent of revision in this chapter illustrates the dynamic character of Agrippa's rewriting process. Rather than simply updating facts, he appears to have substantially reconfigured the argument. Comparison with the partial edition A — which represents an intermediate stage — may reveal whether the additions were introduced gradually or in a single later revision.

Scholarly Perspectives

The ritual and preparatory chapters of Book III describe the moral and procedural conditions that the magician must satisfy before undertaking higher operations. Van der Poel (1997) emphasises that this material reflects genuine religious conviction rather than mere formulaic piety: for Agrippa, ritual purity and moral transformation are constitutive of magical efficacy, not merely customary preliminaries. Miles (2008) reads the detailed ritual instructions as setting up the retraction-paradox of the epilogue: by providing full procedural detail, Agrippa enables — and then appears to withdraw — access to the most powerful operations.
