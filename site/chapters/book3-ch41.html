Book III — Ceremonial Magic — Chapter 41

Of the Virtues of Suffumigations

Quid de homine post mortem opiniones variae
Heavily Expanded

Perrone Compagni — Table of Comparison

1533 edition (3:41): W, 3: 24 + many substantial additions

This chapter, Quid de homine post mortem opiniones variae (“Of the Virtues of Suffumigations”), occupies a significant place in Agrippa's systematic exposition of occult philosophy. In the 1533 Cologne printed edition (De occulta philosophia libri tres, sigla K in Perrone Compagni's critical apparatus), the text represents Agrippa's mature formulation of the ideas it addresses, the product of over two decades of reading, travel, and revision since the composition of the Würzburg draft.

Based on W 3:24 of the Würzburg draft, this chapter was significantly augmented. Perrone Compagni identifies one or more substantial additions, indicating that Agrippa supplemented the original argument with new material, possibly drawn from sources encountered after the 1510 composition.

The extent of revision in this chapter illustrates the dynamic character of Agrippa's rewriting process. Rather than simply updating facts, he appears to have substantially reconfigured the argument. Comparison with the partial edition A — which represents an intermediate stage — may reveal whether the additions were introduced gradually or in a single later revision.
